# Borrower's Guide - Our 2022 Winnebago Travato 59K
> tagline: Everything you need to enjoy the van. Tap any card for step-by-step instructions with photos — or open Quick Reference for the one-screen cheat sheet.
> walkthrough: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bQeor0aaGv8

## [Start Here] Welcome {#welcome} {icon:home}
**Quick:** Thanks for borrowing our van! Skim these cards before you go. Tap any card for full steps and photos, or open the Quick Reference card for a one-screen cheat sheet. Call us anytime — the number is in the Emergency card.
**Steps:**
1. Do a walk-around outside: check tires look inflated, nothing left under the van, all exterior doors/compartments latched.
2. Inside, find the OnePlace monitor panel — this shows battery charge, water tank, and waste tank levels.
3. Locate the fire extinguisher, first-aid kit, and the two exits (side door and cab doors).
4. Confirm the fridge is on and cold before loading food.
5. Read the "Dos & Don'ts" card once — it prevents 90% of problems.
**Tips:**
- When in doubt, take a photo of a switch/valve position BEFORE you change it, so you can put it back.
- The full Winnebago manual and helpful walk-through videos are in the Resources card.

## [Power] Battery System {#battery-system} {icon:bolt}
**Quick:** The house runs on an AGM battery (it powers the 12V things: lights, water pump, fridge). An INVERTER makes 120V power, but ONLY for the TV, the sound bar, and the 2 outlets inside the cabinet above the TV. EVERYTHING else — all other outlets, the A/C, and the microwave — needs the GENERATOR running, the VEHICLE ENGINE running (while driving), or SHORE POWER.
**Steps:**
1. The inverter switch is above the sliding door. To turn it On or Off, press and hold the switch for 1–2 seconds, then release — a green light shows when it's On.
2. Inverter On powers the TV, the sound bar, and the 2 outlets in the cabinet above the TV.
3. For any other outlet, the A/C, or the microwave: run the generator, run the engine (driving), or use shore power.
4. Check the OnePlace panel (above the sliding door) for battery charge and tank levels.
5. Turn the inverter Off when done to save the battery.
**Tips:**
- Easy rule: TV + sound bar + the 2 cabinet-above-TV outlets = inverter/battery. Everything else = generator, engine, or shore power.
- The AGM battery recharges four ways: driving (engine alternator), running the generator, shore power, and the rooftop SOLAR PANELS on sunny days.
- The fridge (12V), lights, water pump, and fans all sip from the battery even when parked — so recharge daily when boondocking.
- If an inverter outlet has no power, the inverter's GFCI may have tripped — press the RESET button on the GFCI outlet, then try again.
**Warnings:**
- AGM batteries should not be run flat — recharge before the battery gets low (drive, generator, solar, or shore power).

## [Power] Generator {#generator} {icon:generator}
**Quick:** The under-floor Onan generator makes the 120V power that runs the A/C, the microwave, and ALL the regular outlets (the inverter only covers the TV, sound bar, and cabinet-above-TV outlet). Start/stop it from the generator switch. If it trips or won't start, use the RESET steps below.
**Steps:**
1. Before starting: turn OFF big 120V loads (A/C, microwave) so the generator starts under light load.
2. Find the generator START/STOP switch (interior, near the monitor panel). Press and HOLD toward START until the generator fires, then release. It may crank a few seconds.
3. Let it warm up ~1–2 minutes before switching on the A/C or microwave.
4. To stop: turn off A/C/microwave first, then hold the switch toward STOP.
**Tips:**
- The generator itself is in a compartment underneath the van. Never run it while sleeping without working CO detectors, and never in an enclosed space.
- Keep at least 1/4 tank of fuel — the generator shares the van's gas tank and will stop to protect the engine's fuel supply.
**Warnings:**
- Generator exhaust contains carbon monoxide, which is deadly. Keep windows/doors on the exhaust side closed and make sure the CO detector is working.
### RESET the Onan generator (won't start, or tripped off)
**Steps:**
1. Turn OFF all 120V loads inside (A/C, microwave, chargers).
2. Go to the generator unit underneath the van (exterior access panel/compartment).
3. On the generator's own face/control area, find the CIRCUIT BREAKER / RESET button (a push-button, often red or black, sometimes labeled "RESET" or a rocker breaker).
4. If it's popped out/tripped, press it firmly IN to reset it.
5. At the generator (or interior switch), press and HOLD the STOP side of the switch for about 3–5 seconds to clear any fault, then release.
6. Now press and HOLD the START/PRIME side until the generator starts, then release once it's running smoothly.
7. If it still won't start, check: fuel level (need ~1/4 tank), and look for a blinking fault light on the generator — count the blinks and match to the Onan operator card in the InfoCase.
**Warnings:**
- If the generator repeatedly trips or won't start after one reset attempt, STOP and call the owner. Do not force it.

## [Power] Plugging In {#shore-power} {icon:plug}
**Quick:** "Shore power" means plugging the van into a campground outlet (30-amp). This runs EVERYTHING (including A/C and microwave) AND recharges the battery. Turn the campground breaker OFF before plugging/unplugging. Power extension cords and outlet adapters are stored in the rear compartment (open the passenger back door — above and beside the water inlets, same place as the water hoses).
**Steps:**
1. Open the PASSENGER BACK DOOR. In the rear compartment above/beside the water tank inlets you'll find the shore cord, power EXTENSION CORDS, OUTLET ADAPTERS, and the water hoses.
2. At the campground pedestal, switch the breaker OFF.
3. Plug the cord into the van's sidewall inlet (driver side) and the other end into the 30-amp pedestal outlet. Use an adapter from the compartment if the outlet doesn't match.
4. Switch the pedestal breaker back ON.
5. You now have full power, and the battery is charging automatically.
**Tips:**
- Everything you need for hookups — power cords, adapters, and water hoses — lives together in that rear compartment behind the passenger back door.
- Use a 30-amp outlet when possible. On a household (15/20-amp) outlet with an adapter, you can't run the A/C and microwave at the same time.
**Warnings:**
- Use the proper heavy-duty cords/adapters from the compartment — don't use a light household extension cord for the main hookup, as undersized cords can overheat and cause a fire.
- Make sure all prongs are fully seated and dry before switching power on.

## [Water] Water & Pump {#water} {icon:water}
**Quick:** Water comes from the onboard fresh tank (turn the WATER PUMP switch ON to get pressure at the faucets) or from a city hose hookup. The pump switch is on the monitor panel and the Water Center panel — an indicator light shows when it's on.
**Steps:**
1. To use tank water: turn the WATER PUMP switch ON (on the monitor panel or Water Center panel). Water flows when you open a faucet.
2. Open a faucet briefly — you'll hear the pump run to build pressure, then it quiets when pressurized.
3. If using a campground water spigot instead, connect a drinking-water hose to the city water inlet — then you don't need the pump.
4. Check the fresh water level on the OnePlace monitor panel.
**Tips:**
- Turn the pump OFF when you leave the van, so a drip or open valve can't drain the tank or flood.
- Conserve water when boondocking: shorter showers, turn off while soaping.

**Steps:**
1. Open the exterior compartment behind the rear passenger-side door to reach the wash station.
2. Make sure the water pump is ON (or you're connected to city water).
3. Connect/pull out the sprayer hose, set the hot/cold mixing valves to a comfortable temperature.
4. Use the trigger/valve on the sprayer to control the water.
5. When done, shut off the valves, drain and stow the hose, and close the compartment.
**Tips:**
- Great for a quick rinse without tracking sand or mud inside the van.
- Turn the water pump OFF afterward so nothing keeps the system pressurized.
**Warnings:**
- In freezing weather, don't leave the sprayer hose connected/full — it can freeze.

2. Confirm the Water Center panel is NOT in "bypass/winterize" (the red lever should be in the normal, not bypass, position).
3. Turn the water heater ON.
4. Wait about 20–30 minutes for the water to heat.
5. Turn it OFF when you're done to conserve power/propane.
**Warnings:**
- NEVER turn on the water heater when it's empty (in bypass or winterized mode) — it can be damaged.

## [Water] Toilet {#toilet} {icon:toilet}
**Quick:** Flush with the foot/hand pedal — a little water first, then flush. OUR TRICK: use a biodegradable bowl liner for solids so we fill the black tank much slower and dump less often. Only RV-safe toilet paper. Nothing else goes in.
**Steps:**
1. For a normal flush: hold the flush lever/pedal — add a little water to the bowl first, do your business, then flush fully so waste clears into the black tank.
2. OWNER'S BOWL-LINER TRICK (to save black tank space when boondocking): before use, drop a biodegradable RV toilet bowl liner bag into the bowl. After solids-only use, seal the bag and dispose in the trash instead of flushing — this keeps the black tank from filling quickly.
3. Add a little water and a scoop/pod of holding-tank treatment after dumping to control odor.
**Tips:**
- Keep a small stock of RV-safe toilet paper and bowl liners in the bathroom cabinet.
- Liquids-only flushing between liner uses keeps things fresh without filling the tank.
**Warnings:**
- Do NOT flush regular facial/household tissue, wipes (even "flushable"), sanitary products, or any non-dissolving items — they clog the tank.
- Do NOT pour bleach or harsh chemicals in the toilet — they damage the seals.

## [Water] Gray/Black Tanks {#dumping} {icon:drop}
**Quick:** When tanks are full (check the monitor panel), dump at a dump station. Connect the sewer hose, empty BLACK first, then GRAY (gray rinses the hose). Never leave valves open while parked. Wear the gloves in the wet-bay.
**Steps:**
1. Check tank levels on the OnePlace monitor panel before dumping.
2. Put on the disposable gloves. Remove the sewer hose from its storage compartment.
3. Remove the cap from the sewage drain outlet and firmly attach the sewer hose. Put the other end securely into the dump station opening.
4. Open the BLACK tank valve first with a quick pull. Let it fully drain, then close it.
5. Open the GRAY tank valve next (this rinses black residue from the hose). Close it when empty.
6. Rinse/flush if a rinse connection is available, store the hose, add tank treatment through the toilet.
**Tips:**
- Dump before travel days when possible — you don't want to haul the extra weight.
- Rule of thumb: BLACK before GRAY, never both at once.
**Warnings:**
- Never open the gray valve before the black is drained and closed, or you'll back sewage into the gray side.

## [Appliances] Refrigerator {#fridge} {icon:fridge}
**Quick:** The fridge runs automatically on 12V from the house battery (no generator needed). Turn it on and set the dial around 3–4. Give it a few hours to cool before loading lots of food. Leave it on the whole trip.
**Steps:**
1. Turn the fridge power ON and set the thermostat to about 3–4 (midrange).
2. Let it cool for 3–4 hours (or overnight) before fully loading with food.
3. To turn off (end of trip), set the thermostat to zero/off and prop the door open to prevent mold.
**Tips:**
- Pre-chill food before loading; the fridge cools better when it's not cooling warm items.
- Keep the door closed on hot days; open it as little as possible.

**Steps:**
1. Get 120V power first: start the generator, plug into shore power, or run the engine. The microwave does NOT work on the inverter.
2. Use it like a normal microwave.
3. If it won't power on, you don't have generator/shore/engine power — start the generator or plug in.
**Warnings:**
- The microwave will NOT run on the inverter/battery — it needs the generator, shore power, or the engine running.

- Turn the propane OFF while driving and when refueling (see Propane & Stove for safety).

## [Cooling & Heating] Roof A/C {#roof-ac} {icon:snowflake}
**Quick:** The roof A/C is the main way to cool a parked van. It needs REAL 120V power: the GENERATOR running, SHORE POWER, or the VEHICLE ENGINE running (while driving). It will NOT run on the inverter/battery. Select Low Cool / High Cool / Auto and set the temperature. There's also a Bluetooth app to control it.
**Steps:**
1. Get 120V power first: start the generator (see Generator card), plug into shore power, or run the engine. The A/C does NOT work on the inverter.
2. Press the selector to "Low Cool", "High Cool", or "Auto".
3. Set the temperature to comfortable. The A/C cycles on/off to hold the temperature.
4. Aim the louvers where you want airflow.
**Tips:**
- On cooler nights (below ~75°F), set the temp to the midpoint, not the coldest — running it too cold can ice up the coil and stop cooling.
- If it ices up and airflow drops, run the fan on High until the ice clears.
- The A/C can be controlled from a phone via its Bluetooth app (see Resources).
**Warnings:**
- The A/C will NOT run on the inverter/battery — if it won't start, you need the generator, shore power, or the engine running.
- If the GENERATOR is running but the A/C still won't start, the BREAKER on the generator unit (underneath the van) may be tripped — reset it (see the Generator card's RESET steps).

## [Cooling & Heating] Dash A/C {#dash-ac} {icon:steering}
**Quick:** The dash A/C is the van's automotive air conditioning — it only works while the ENGINE is running, so it's for staying cool on the road. It's small and weak for the living space, so don't rely on it to cool a parked van (use the roof A/C for that).
**Steps:**
1. Start the engine.
2. Use the dash climate controls (fan, temperature, A/C button) just like a car.
3. To cool a parked van, use the Roof A/C instead.
**Tips:**
- When it's really hot, run BOTH the dash A/C (engine) and the roof A/C to cool the van down faster.

## [Cooling & Heating] Heater (Hybrid) {#heater} {icon:heat}
**Quick:** The heater is a hybrid (Truma) — it makes warm forced air AND hot water, and can run on PROPANE, on 120V ELECTRIC (shore/generator), or both ("Mix"). Use the round control panel near the monitor panel: set the room temperature and pick the energy source.
**Steps:**
1. For propane heat, make sure the LP (propane) switch above the side door is ON.
2. On the heater's round control panel (near the monitor panel), press the rotary knob for ~3 seconds to turn it on.
3. Turn the knob to set the room temperature. Use the menu to choose the energy mode: Gas (propane), Electric (needs shore/generator), or Mix.
4. The same system heats your hot water — set the hot water temperature in the menu too.
**Tips:**
- Gas (propane) mode works anywhere with no hookup. Electric mode needs shore power or the generator.
- On very cold nights, "Mix" uses both for faster, steadier heat.
**Warnings:**
- When running propane heat overnight, keep a vent cracked and make sure the CO detector is working.

## [Entertainment] Sound Bar {#soundbar} {icon:speaker}

## [Comfort] Awning {#awning} {icon:awning}
**Quick:** The Carefree awning control has two rockers: LEFT = ON/OFF, RIGHT = RETRACT/EXTEND. Two rules: (1) the LEFT switch must be ON before extend/retract will work, and (2) the PARKING BRAKE must be ON. Safety features: if you release the parking brake while the awning is out, OR if the wind gets too strong, the awning retracts itself automatically. There's an awning LIGHT switch right next to it.
**Steps:**
1. Set the PARKING BRAKE — the awning will not operate without it.
2. On the awning control, flip the LEFT switch to ON.
3. Hold the RIGHT switch toward EXTEND to open the awning (or RETRACT to close it); release when it's where you want it.
4. To turn the awning light on/off, use the LIGHT switch next to the awning control.
5. When you're done adjusting, you can flip the LEFT switch back to OFF.
**Tips:**
- Extend it partway to shade the side windows — it helps keep the van cooler on hot days.
**Warnings:**
- ALWAYS retract the awning before driving, and in strong wind, rain, or storms.
- Auto-retract: releasing the parking brake with the awning out will make it close on its own, and so will high wind — keep hands and objects clear of the awning arms.

## [Driving] Leveling {#leveling} {icon:level}
**Quick:** Park on the flattest spot you can find. Use the leveling blocks under the tires for small slopes (drive up onto them). Set the parking brake, chock a wheel if on any incline. A level van means the fridge works well and you sleep better.
**Steps:**
1. Pick the flattest available spot. Check level using the bubble level (or a phone level app) inside.
2. For minor slopes, place the leveling blocks in front of/behind the low-side tires and slowly drive onto them.
3. Set the parking brake firmly.
4. On any slope, place a wheel chock for extra safety.
**Tips:**
- Level side-to-side matters most for sleeping comfort and the fridge.

## [Driving] Driving Basics {#driving} {icon:steering}
**Quick:** It drives like a tall van. Watch your HEIGHT (about 9'4" — mind low clearances, drive-throughs, garages, tree branches). Use the mirrors and back-up camera. Take corners wide. Before moving: everything stowed, steps/antenna in, cords disconnected.
**Steps:**
1. PRE-DRIVE CHECK: inside items stowed, roof vents/antenna down, TV antenna down, steps in, windows closed, fridge latched.
2. OUTSIDE: shore cord unplugged and stored, water hose disconnected, all compartment doors latched, nothing left behind.
3. Adjust mirrors and seat. Note the height (~9'4") for clearances.
4. Drive smoothly — it's taller and heavier than a car, so brake earlier and take turns wide.
**Warnings:**
- Low clearances are the #1 rental RV damage. When unsure of a clearance, DON'T risk it — find another route.

## [Safety] Propane & Stove {#propane} {icon:propane}
**Quick:** Propane powers the stove (and furnace). Turn the propane valve on to cook, off when done and when driving/refueling. If you ever smell gas: no flames, no switches — open windows, turn off propane, and get everyone out.
**Steps:**
1. To cook: make sure the propane supply valve is ON, light the burner per the stove instructions.
2. Turn burners OFF and the propane supply OFF when finished.
3. Turn propane OFF while driving and always at gas stations.
**Warnings:**
- If you smell propane (rotten-egg smell): do NOT touch light switches or create sparks. Open windows/doors, shut off the propane, exit the van, and don't return until it's clear.
- Keep the CO/propane detector powered — never disable it.

## [Help] Dos & Don'ts {#dos-donts} {icon:checklist}
**Quick:** A quick cheat sheet of the things that keep the van happy.
**Tips:**
- DO turn the inverter and water pump OFF when you leave the van.

## [Help] Emergency {#emergency} {icon:phone}
**Quick:** Save the owner's number in your phone before you leave. For roadside issues use the roadside assistance number. In a real emergency call 911. Breaker/reset locations are listed below.
**Steps:**
1. OWNER (call/text anytime): __________  (fill in)
2. Roadside assistance / insurance: __________  (fill in policy #)
3. If a 120V outlet is dead: check the inverter GFCI reset, then the house 120V breakers.
4. If the generator won't run: see the Generator RESET card.
5. If nothing electrical works: check the main house breaker and the battery charge (may just need charging — drive, run the generator, or plug in).
**Tips:**
- House 120V circuit breakers and fuses are in the load center (interior panel) — labeled inside the door.

## [Help] FAQ {#faq} {icon:checklist}
**Quick:** Quick answers to the questions borrowers ask most (thanks to the Travato Owners community for many of these).
**FAQ:**
- Why does the OnePlace tank/battery gauge seem to drop fast? :: It reads the next level DOWN as soon as you use a little. Water/gray/black show Full, 2/3, 1/3, Empty; LP shows 3/4, 1/2, 1/4, E. So "3/4" really means "somewhere between 3/4 and full." It's normal.
- What runs on the battery (DC) vs. needs generator/shore (AC)? :: On battery (DC): fridge, lights, TV, sound bar, ceiling/Max fans, water pump, and the OnePlace display. Needs 120V AC (generator, shore, or engine): the roof A/C and the microwave.
- Water sprays from the shower connection on the wall when I flush the toilet — why? :: The wall shower valve is left ON. Turn that shower valve OFF; otherwise running the water pump over-pressurizes that line and water comes out the connection.
- I can't insert or remove the shower hose in the wall port. :: There's pressure in the line. Turn the water pump OFF (and city water off), then open an inside faucet to relieve pressure. Then push in the chrome sleeve to release, or push the quick-connect in to insert.
- It's really hot — how do I get it cool? :: Manage the sun: put in the windshield/cab-door covers, close the shades on the sunny side, and extend the awning to shade the windows. Run the cab A/C (engine) plus the roof A/C to catch up, and use 12V/USB fans. The A/C only cools about 20°F below the outside temperature.
- Should I use the onboard tank or "city water" hookup? :: Most owners use the onboard fresh tank and just switch the water pump ON only while using water. Bonus: if you ever hear the pump running when no tap is open, that's your early warning of a leak.
- Where's the light switch for the driver's-side dump/service area? :: It's an unlabeled switch on the back wall near the water pump switch (owners call it the "GSDL"). The switch on the other side of the pump switch runs the back-wall light.
- What's in the InfoCase and should I keep it in the van? :: Yes—keep it in the van. It has the owner's manuals, the ProMaster manual, the window sticker, and a component sheet with model/serial numbers you'll want if you ever call for help on the road.
